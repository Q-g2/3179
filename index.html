Worldwide Continents Environment Condition 2023

Temperature

The earth is getting warmer and warmer, the rise of the surface temperature contributed by various factors resulted from human activities, in particular the emission of greenhouse gas like carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide.

These gases trap the heat in the atmosphere,creating a 'greenhouse effect' that leads to various climate change. This phenomenon results melting ice, rising sea leavels, and more extreme weather events such as heatwaves, drought, and heavy rainfall.

The impact on ecosystems is profound, endangering all living organism on the planet,Reducing emissions through energy efficiently and exploring renewable energy sources are critical to mitigate the issue.

Visibility

Visibility of the atmosphere affect the distance at which any object or light can be discerned. It can be influenced by several factors, such as air pollution, fog, rain, snow, and other weather conditions. In the context of air quality, poor visibility can often be a sign of high levels of air pollutants, such as particulate matter or smog.

The chart below shows that most countries across all continents has poorer visibility with distance under 10km, there are small amount of countries which located within America continent region and pacific region has better visibility, with visibility distance reach to more than double of 10km.

Wind Speed

Wind speed is a measure of the velocity of the air on a point on Earth's surface. It is reported in kilometers per hour (km/h) in the provided dataset. Wind speed can have an impact on various aspects of human activities which include aviation, marine navigation and construction.

The chart below shows that Australia contient generally have fairly high Wind speed in both August and September of 2023. America continent has highest Wind speed in August among all recorded continents globally.

UV Index

UV index is a measure of the strength of sunburn-producing ultraviolet radiation. The higher the index, the greater the potential for harm.

An index of 1 to 2 is considered "low" risk of exposure, while 8 to 10 signifies "very high" risk, and 11 or more indicates an "extreme" risk.

The ozone layer absorbs most of the sun's harmful UV radiation. Thining of the ozone layer due to cheminal pollutants has led to increased UV radiation levels.

The heatmap chart below shows UV index of all major continents during in either the days of August or September in 2023. According to the chart, Most days of the continent of Africa, Atlantic, Europe and Indian have very low UV index of 1 to 2. In comparison, Pacific region has very fairly high UV index of 5 and above most days in the month of August or September in 2023.

Humidity

Humidity refers to the concentration of water vapor present in the air. It affects weather pattern. From the chart, highest Humidity continent is Atlantic, on average 82%.

High Humidity can lead to condensation and precipitation. It can impact air quality by interacting with pollutants.

HighHumidity can exacerbate the presence of certain pollutants, such as ozone, which can be more harmful on humid days. It also affects the concentration and dispersal of airborne.

EPA Index VS PM2.5

Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Index is Air Quality Index, it indicates the severity of air pollution. Different country use difference indices. The one used here is USA EPA index.

PM2.5 refers to particulate matter that is 2.5 micrometers in diameter or smaller. Sources of PM2.5 include a variety of combustion activities, such as motor vehicles, power plants, wood burning, forest fires, and certain industrial processes.

The higher the EPA index and the higher the PM2.5 level in various contients across the globe during the period of August and September in 2023.

Most countries across all listed continents have fairly good air quality with EPA index less than 2 and PM2.5 below 200 level.
